Personal Storm Cloud

Visualising Your Carbon Footprint in Real Time

Our Concept

Imagine a future where climate change is no longer an abstract concept hidden in data charts, but a visible part of daily life. In this world, every individual is followed by a Personal Storm Cloud—a visual representation of their carbon footprint. This cloud changes shape, colour and intensity based on your daily habits such as transport choices, food consumption, energy usage and shopping patterns.

A person who commutes by car would have a dark, swirling cloud overhead, while a cyclist or public transport user would be accompanied by a light, translucent cloud. Individuals could track their impact in real time and make behavioural changes to improve their "storm cloud"—like switching to renewable energy or reducing waste.

This technology would draw on live data from personal devices and urban environmental sensors. It merges biofeedback and lifestyle tracking into a single visual scene. It raises important questions:
Would we act differently if our environmental footprint was visible to everyone?
Could personal responsibility, made public, accelerate climate action?

The Personal Storm Cloud provokes a new emotional connection with climate data. Instead of numbers on a screen, the environmental cost of our daily lives becomes tangible and impossible to ignore.

Interaction & Technology

The Personal Storm Cloud uses wearable augmented reality glasses or drone projection to create a visible and reactive representation of your current carbon footprint. It’s powered by real-time data from your phone, smart home devices, and city-wide sensors. The cloud responds instantly to your actions—growing darker when you drive, shrinking when you walk, and changing colour based on your energy source.
